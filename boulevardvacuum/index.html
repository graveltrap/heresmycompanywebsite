The Shop's Story

A boulevard institution since the year after the war

When this shop first opened its doors in 1946, families brought in machines they intended to keep for a lifetime — and we helped them do exactly that. Nearly eight decades later, the trade has all but vanished from the city, but the workbench on Pico is still busy. Grandmothers' Singers come in for tune-ups. Modern uprights leave running like new.

We are repairers by conviction: we believe a good machine deserves a long life, and that honest service beats a landfill every time. That philosophy has carried Boulevard Vacuum & Sewing through four generations of customers — and we intend to be here for the fifth.
